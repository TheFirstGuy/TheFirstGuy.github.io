FPGA Based Neural Network

For my senior project I am leading a team to design an artificial neural network on a field programmable gate array (FPGA). More specifically, we intend to create a multi-layer perceptron (MLP) architecture that is capable of solving a variety of classification problems efficiently, while also being easily scalable. If time permits we would ideally would prefer to create an open source library to allow others to build upon our work.

Currently, we plan to implement out design on a Nexys 4 DDR board that is powered by a Xilinx Artix-7 chip. Although not the most powerful FPGA available, we have chosen it due to our familiarity with the board, its low cost, and integrated sensors. The design of the MLP will be done on the register transfer level using VHDL. Although we were trained to use the Xilinx ISE 14.7 software, we are considering moving over to the newer Xilinx Vivado. However, this will be dependent on how long we believe it would take to reorient ourselves.

Neural networks on FPGAs present several challenges. Some of these challenges include, but are not limited to:

  • Managing the connections of synapse between neurons (activators)
  • Implementing multiplication in an efficient manner
  • Implementing the sigmoid function used in back propagation in an efficient manner
  • Pipelining forward activation and back propagation simultaneously
  • Exploiting neural network inherent parallelism
These challenges, along with many others will be tackled over the next few months. I look forward to posting updates as we overcome these challenges.
